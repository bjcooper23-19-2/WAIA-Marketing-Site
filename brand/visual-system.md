# WAIA Visual System

## Brand idea

**Make the next move visible.**

This is the organising principle behind the Open Wayfinder identity. It supports both sides of WAIA:

- participants understand why they are there, what they need to do and what better practice looks like
- organisations can see adoption signals, attention areas and the next practical action

## Identity

### Primary mark

The Open Wayfinder is an open W formed from one continuous guided path.

Use the gradient mark on operating navy and other dark surfaces. Use the deep-teal mark on white or pale surfaces. Use one-colour variants where reproduction or contrast requires them.

### Clear space

Keep clear space around the mark equal to at least one quarter of the mark width. Do not place text, borders or other symbols inside that area.

### Minimum size

- standalone mark: 20px minimum digital height
- compact lockup: 28px minimum digital height
- full horizontal lockup: 44px minimum digital height

### Do not

- place the mark inside a shield or closed frame
- add an arrowhead to the rising path
- add glow strong enough to reduce edge clarity
- stretch, rotate or redraw the mark
- use bright cyan text on pale backgrounds
- combine the WAIA mark with the Nineteen Point Two mark in the same primary lockup

## Colour roles

### Operating navy `#081220`

Use for navigation, product shell, high-level context and dark brand surfaces.

### Deep teal `#0D7377`

Use for primary actions, links, headings and emphasis on light surfaces.

### Bright cyan `#22D3EE`

Use for active states, focus, selected paths and directional illumination on dark surfaces. Do not use for body text on white or pale cyan.

### Clarity white `#FFFFFF`

Use for instruction, expected action, decision content and important explanation.

### Neutral light `#F2F5F7`

Use for supporting information and lower-priority surfaces.

### Text colours

- primary light-surface text: `#0F172A`
- secondary light-surface text: `#475569`
- primary dark-surface text: `#F8FAFC`
- secondary dark-surface text: `#CBD5E1`

## Typography

Inter remains the WAIA interface typeface.

- headings: Inter 600 to 800
- body: Inter 400 to 500
- labels: Inter 600, restrained uppercase, modest tracking
- avoid excessive letter spacing in the WAIA wordmark and navigation

## Surface roles

### Operating surface

Dark navy. Used for navigation, organisational context, product framing and status.

### Clarity surface

White with dark text, a restrained border and minimal shadow. Used when the user must understand or act.

### Supporting surface

Pale neutral with dark text. Used for explanatory material that is useful but not the immediate instruction.

### Action surface

Deep teal on light backgrounds. Bright cyan should remain an accent rather than the whole button fill unless it passes contrast with dark text.

### Caution and risk

Use semantic amber and red sparingly. A coloured border, label and icon are usually sufficient. Do not flood large panels with low-contrast tints.

## WAIA Clarity Panel

The Clarity Panel is the signature light-surface component shared by the marketing site and the WAIA product.

It should contain only the parts relevant to the context, selected from:

1. Why you are here
2. What you will do
3. Why it matters
4. What is expected
5. What you will take away
6. Your next move

### Visual treatment

- white background
- dark primary text
- deep-teal labels or rule
- small Open Wayfinder mark
- no decorative gradient behind body copy
- clear separation between sections
- one dominant action at most

## Marketing-site application

The marketing site may use more editorial spacing and larger headings than the product. It must still share:

- the same mark and lockups
- the same colour roles
- the same contrast rules
- the same button hierarchy
- the same meaning for white clarity surfaces

## Product alignment

The product rollout will follow after the marketing pilot is approved. The product should replace the Nineteen Point Two mark in its shell with the WAIA mark, then adopt the Clarity Panel in session opening and next-action contexts.
